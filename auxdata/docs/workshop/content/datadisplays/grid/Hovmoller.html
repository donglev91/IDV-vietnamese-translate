Hovmöller diagrams excel at displaying data as a function of time and location.

The Hovmöller diagram was invented by Danish meteorologist Ernest Aabo Hovmöller (1912-2008). Typically, time is displayed on the ordinate (y-axis) and geolocation on the abscissa (x-axis). They are commonly used in meteorology and oceanography.

Let's create a Hovmöller of sea surface temperature (SST) from the Pacific Ocean to observe El Niño/La Niña-Southern Oscillation.

  • In the [ht::dialog Data Choosers] tree's [ht::param URLs] node, enter the [ht::dataset dods://www.esrl.noaa.gov/psd/thredds/dodsC/Datasets/noaa.ersst/sst.mnmean.nc] URL and click the [ht::command Add Source] button.
  • In the [ht::dialog Field Selector]'s [ht::command Data Sources] panel, right-click the SST datasource and select [ht::command Properties] dialog.
  • Click on the [ht::tab Times] tab, select the [ht::command Use Selected] menu item and select a time range from January 1997 to December 1998 and click the [ht::command Apply] button.
  • Click on the [ht::tab Spatial Subset] tab and select a region in the equatorial Pacific Ocean from Mexico to Hawaii and click the [ht::command OK] button.
  • In the [ht::command Fields] panel, select the [ht::param Monthly Means of Sea Surface Temperature] field.
  • In the [ht::command Displays] panel, expand the [ht::param Hovmoller] tab and select [ht::display Time-Longitude (Contours)] then click the [ht::command Create Display] button.
  • Modify the contour labeling by clicking in the Contour [ht::command Change] button. Choose a contour interval of 1 degree Celsius.
  • Modify the color table by clicking the Color table [ht::command default] button. Change the range from 15 to 32.
  • Can you see the El Niño/La Niña-Southern Oscillation?
